Background of Gewandhaus the building and its orchestra in Leipzig

Gewandhaus is a German word meaning 'Cloth Hall or house of dresses', a part of the building was used as a place for treading clothes. The building had undergone construction three times since it had been used as concert hall in 1781. The Second Gewandhaus, so-called 'Neues Gewandhaus' was opened in 1884 but then was destucted by bombing in the World War Two in 1847. The third Gewandhaus, the one in Leipzig now was opened in 1981.[3] The root of the orchestra can be traced back as early as ... 1479. The members of the orchestra were appointed by the Leipzig City Council and they remained in civic service until 1840. The orchestra then became the Gewandhaus Orchestra in 1781 after the renovation as the Gewandhaus concert hall building. [4] Gewandhaus the concert hall isone of the famous and the earliest concert in Europe. The concet hall is the venue for the premiere of the following masterpiece that include Beethoven's Fifth PianoCOncerto "Emperor", Mendelssohn's violin Concerto, and the next two masterpieces were conducted by Mendelssohn-Schubert's C major Symphony "The Great" and Schumann's First Symphony "Spring". [5] One of the reasons of why Gewandhaus the concert hall able to attract fampus musicians to have their works be performed there, was because of its excellent acoustic sound quality according to Dr.Erich Kässmayer in the interview.

Summary of the Gewandhaus the building and its orchestra

The name 'Gewandhaus' is not only referring to the vuilding itself but also the site as well. And there was a building in that site of Gewandhaus was used as a storage of artillery in the middle ages, and that building became adoptable since it was kept empty in the late 18th century, and that building was perfect for transforming as concert hall because of its bit roof and the wooden panels that preoduce excellent acoustic to the room. And the compact size of holding 400 seats also takes credit of its acoustic condition.
