Mendelssohn's musical contribution

Felix Mendelssohn was appointed as the Music Director in Gewandhaus orchestra for two period of time (1835-1843), (1845-1847). He died in his Leipzig home in 1847.

He trained up and enlarged his Gewandhaus Orchestra

Under the influence in the social aspect and in the music developmennt aspect, it is not hard to predict the development of the Gewandhaus Orchestra, and indeed, it had a change on its orchestral player number. In the orchestral player numbers comparison table (Fig.1)[14], just before Mendelssohn became th music Director in 1833, there were 34 members in the orchestra, and the number of members increased to 47 by the time of 1839 with more violins, cellos, double basses and Timpani.

Under the directorship of Mendelssohn, the orchestra was no longer rely on the concertmaster for beating time, interestingly, at that time, 'the concertmaster sometimes beating times, sometimes playing, directed instrumental works from his desk.' (Friedrich Schmidt who is an architect commented on Mendelssohn's conducting style) Then the music director would take over time beating when the singing ocurred.

Besides the leadership on time beating, Mendelssohn ... also set out some rules in his conducting practice such as, strict discipline, careful rehearsing, baton conducting.[16] And he designed a player seating model for his orchestra that suggest the basic of inner-aural image when he thinks in orchestral forces.[17] The seating model is shown in Fig. 2 [18] Ferdinand David, the concertmaster in Mendelssohn's orchestra highly appreciated on his model, he says' The orchestral arrangment in this admirable hall is so good, communication between the conductor and each member of the orchestra is so easy, the players... have been trained by Mendelssohn to such a poutch of disciplice and concertration that two rehearsal suffuced for putting together a long programme.[19]

His motivation on reviving historical compositions

Mendelssohn rasied historical awareness to his orchestra, he demanded his orchestra for the need of a 'prodominantly non-vibrate tone.'He states that 'the orchestral violinist should "avoid every addition of turns, doubleturns, shakes or'likewise all artificial shifting.'[20](Dacid Milsom, 2008)

His idea on reviving historical works such as J.S. Bach etc. Fig.3 [21] shows an example of Mendelssohn's programming of historical concerts between 1835 to 1840.

Mendelssohn's audience

During 1815-1850, Leipzig grew from 45,000 to 63,000, and up to 2010 population in Leipzig was increased to 522,883. [22] So proprtionately, the audience for concert was higher in the nineteenth century. [23]Mendelssohn's educational desire of promting histocial repertoire characterized by his Bourgeois audience, 'The Englightment and the early nineteenth century produced diverse viewpoints on education, which framed the bourgeois educational experience.'[24]Friedrich Schleiermacher (1768-1824) commented 'The role of rising education in creating national cultural unity.'[25] So, under the social climax in rising nationality unity and significance of education, that created a solid ground of Mendelssohn's audience.
